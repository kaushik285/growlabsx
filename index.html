Peptides Science

Understanding the science behind growth enhancement

🧪

Growth Hormone Peptides

Growth hormone-releasing peptides (GHRPs) are synthetic compounds that stimulate the pituitary gland to produce more growth hormone naturally.

These peptides work by mimicking ghrelin, a hormone that signals the body to release growth hormone, particularly during sleep and exercise.

  • Increases natural HGH production by up to 300%
  • Promotes bone density and length during growth phases
  • Enhances protein synthesis for muscle development
  • Improves sleep quality for better recovery
  • Supports collagen production for joint health

Hormones & Growth

The biological mechanisms behind height development

Key Growth Hormones

Multiple hormones work synergistically to regulate growth and development throughout childhood and adolescence.

  • Growth Hormone (GH): Primary hormone for linear growth, secreted by pituitary gland
  • IGF-1: Insulin-like growth factor stimulated by GH, promotes bone and tissue growth
  • Thyroid Hormones: Regulate metabolism and are essential for normal growth
  • Sex Hormones: Testosterone and estrogen contribute to growth spurts during puberty
  • Cortisol: Stress hormone that can inhibit growth when chronically elevated
